Frequently Asked Questions

Click to reveal answers!

What should I bring to TigerHacks?

Bring your computer and chargers, as well as hygiene items such as deodorant, toothpaste, and a toothbrush! Also make sure to bring copies of your resume!

What will be at TigerHacks?

There'll be plenty of food and swag! You'll also be able to meet amazing mentors who will help you out! (There are no showers though, so make sure to bring deodorant!)

Will there be internship opportunities?

Yes! We will be holding a career fair, so make sure to bring copies of your resume!

I have a food allergy. Will there be accomodations?

Yes! When you register, let us know and we will have food options available for you!

Will there be places to sleep?

We have 20 mattresses, first come first serve. Make sure to bring your own pillows and blankets!

How large can teams be?

Teams can have anywhere from 1 to 4 members!

Where is TigerHacks held? Where can I park?

It's at Lafferre Hall! For parking, go to the Turner Avenue Garage!

Do I have to know how to code to attend?

Nope! We have an entire category dedicated to beginners, so even if you know nothing you could still win! We also have a hardware category, if hardware interests you more!

I have a question that isn't here!

Feel free to message us on our Discord!

SCHEDULE & PRIZES

Developer Category

The developer category is the granddaddy of all the categories. While some might call it the general category, this division is for students that are not beginners and are not particularly concerned with winning any of the subcategories.

First place prize is a $400 Visa gift card per team member! Second place prize is 4 INSIGNIA Fire TVs! Third place prize is 4 Nintendo Switch Lites!

Check out the rubric here!

Beginner Category

If you are new to programming, this category is for you. This category is designed for Mizzou's CS 1050, CS 2050 or IT 1040 students with limited programming knowledge. Only know C or Python from your classes? This category is for you. See if you can cleverly solve a complex problem with simple technology or dive into a new technology you’ve never seen before. Participants of this category are not able to compete in the developer category.

First place prize is Bose Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones! Second place prize is Wireless Speakers!

Check out the rubric here!

Business Category

Do you have an amazing idea for a media business? Now's your chance to make it a reality! Get your programming hats on and turn your idea into a usable product!

First place prize is 4 Apple Watch SEs!

Check out the rubric here!

Game Category

In this category, participants are tasked with developing a game that engages with the theme of media in captivating and meaningful ways. Your game can take inspiration from various aspects of media, including journalism, social media, movies, music, literature, and more. It's an opportunity to explore how media influences our world, both positively and negatively, and how it can be used as a tool for storytelling, education, or entertainment.

First place prize is 4 Keychron K8 Pro Wireless Keyboards! Second place prize is 4 $100 Steam gift cards!

Check out the rubric here!

Hardware Category

Unlike other categories, the "Innovative Hardware Creations" category is fairly open-ended, allowing participants to explore their hardware interests without any predefined theme constraints. Whether you're passionate about robotics, IoT devices, wearable technology, or any other hardware-related field, this category provides a platform to bring your ideas to life. Cobble together a hardware device with cardboard, microcontrollers, sensors, 3d printers, and more! Only FOUR TEAMS may participate, first come first serve!

First place prize is 4 Ender 3 3D Printers!

Check out the rubric here!

Raffle Prizes

Even if you don't win a category, you can still win from our raffle! Win a $25 Amazon gift card, a Polaroid Now, a SteelSeries Arctis Nova Pro, or an Elgato Stream Deck!
